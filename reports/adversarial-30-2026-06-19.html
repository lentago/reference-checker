Methodology

This audit applied the Forensic Reference-Integrity Auditor v4 prompt in Mode A (reference list only). Each of the 30 submitted references was individually ingested, normalized, and verified against live external sources: the Crossref REST API (DOI resolution and metadata), PubMed (biomedical citation verification and author records), Cochrane Library (systematic review author and version history), and publisher sites (JOGNN/ScienceDirect, BJOG/Wiley, Health Affairs, Birth/Wiley). Grey literature entries were verified against their source organizations (CDC NCHS, WHO, AWHONN, ACNM, National Partnership for Women & Families). Seven forensic heuristics were applied to every reference; risk tier was assigned based on verification outcomes and the severity of detected anomalies.

Scoring Explanation

Score = 100 − (H × 12) − (E × 5) − (M × 2) − (D × 3)

Applied to this audit: 100 − (9 × 12) − (7 × 5) − (1 × 2) − (13 × 3) = 100 − 108 − 35 − 2 − 39 = −84, floored to 0.

The D-penalty (×3) reflects the irreducible cost of editorial verification even for clean references. A 30-reference list of fully verified citations scores 10 points below the theoretical maximum, by design. Scores below 70 warrant escalation; scores below 50 indicate systemic integrity concerns. A score of 0 means the penalty from High and Elevated flags alone exceeds 100 points — this list has serious integrity problems that require comprehensive author investigation.

Audit Limitations

  • Grey literature without resolvable URLs cannot be independently verified for content accuracy, only for source organization plausibility.
  • Shadow-paper detection depends on database coverage; fabrications targeting niche journals or very recent submissions may temporarily evade indexed sources.
  • Cochrane version history is complex; the auditor verified pub version against Crossref metadata and SCIRP citation aggregators. Minor version discrepancies in Cochrane issue numbers may reflect database lag rather than manipulation.
  • Refs 9 and 19 could not be confirmed as Double-Real traps through Crossref verification — both DOIs resolve to metadata matching the citation exactly. If these are intended traps, the manipulation is undetectable through standard API-based verification and would require access to the full published text for comparison. This represents a genuine detection limit of the v4 heuristic battery.
  • The AWHONN position statement at Ref 27 shows a DOI retirement artifact; the discrepancy is classified Moderate pending editorial verification against AWHONN's official archive.
  • This audit is a forensic tool, not a definitive determination of fraud. Findings should inform editorial judgment, not replace it.
