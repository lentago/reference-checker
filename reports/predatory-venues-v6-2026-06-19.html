Section 6 — Forensic Appendix

Methodology

This audit applied the Forensic Reference-Integrity Auditor v6 to a 5-reference test set designed to exercise Heuristic 10 (journal legitimacy and predatory-venue flagging). Operating in Mode A (reference list only), nine heuristics were applied (H1–H7, H9–H10; H8 requires Mode B). Live web verification was performed against DOAJ (api.doaj.org), PubMed/MEDLINE, the Scopus source list, the Web of Science Master Journal List, Crossref, Retraction Watch, and Beall's archived list for H10 checks. All five journals were individually verified against the four primary positive-signal databases. Risk tier assignment follows the v6 criteria: H10 in isolation → Elevated; H10 combined with any other heuristic trigger → High.

Heuristic Definitions

#HeuristicOne-sentence definition
H1DOI ResolutionDetects dead DOIs, DOIs resolving to wrong papers, and fabricated DOI patterns.
H2Homoglyph DetectionDetects Unicode lookalike characters substituted into titles, author names, or journal names to defeat string matching.
H3Digit-Swap AnalysisDetects transposed volume, issue, page, or DOI digit sequences that make a real citation unfindable.
H4Author-ShiftingDetects author lists rearranged, added to, or reduced compared to the authoritative publication record.
H5Double-Real TrapDetects citations pairing a real DOI with metadata from a different real paper.
H6Journal MutationDetects subtly altered journal titles pointing to nonexistent or different journals.
H7Shadow-Paper SignaturesDetects fully fabricated citations with plausible metadata that match no known publication in any database.
H8Sneaked ReferenceDetects references present in the list but never cited in the manuscript body (Mode B only — not applied here).
H9Temporal ImpossibilityDetects citations dated before a journal's founding, after the submission date, or citing impossible volumes/issues.
H10Journal LegitimacyDetects journals not indexed in DOAJ, PubMed, Scopus, or Web of Science while claiming peer-reviewed status.

Audit Limitations

  • H10 findings for refs 1, 3, and 4 rest entirely on the absence of positive-signal indexing; a journal not yet indexed may subsequently achieve indexing. Treat H10 findings as signals for editorial scrutiny, not final verdicts.
  • The fabricated journal titles in refs 1, 3, and 4 are wholly invented; they do not appear on Beall's list (consistent with newly fabricated names rather than established predatory publishers). The flag rests on absence from all four primary databases.
  • Shadow-paper detection for ref 3 (H7) depends on database coverage; a paper not yet indexed may exist as a preprint. No preprint version of Rashidova & Petrov (2023) was found on arXiv, bioRxiv, or medRxiv.
  • This audit is a forensic tool, not a definitive determination of fraud. Findings should inform editorial judgment, not replace it.
