Methodology

This audit applied the Forensic Reference-Integrity Auditor v6 to the reference list of a real published article (Ahmadinezhad et al., BMC Pregnancy and Childbirth, 2024) to verify scoring calibration and false-positive rate under v6. All 30 references were sourced from the Crossref-deposited cited-works list and curated in test-sets/real-articles/ahmadinezhad-2024.md. Two known Crossref deposit errors were documented in the test-set provenance (Ref 11 corrected DOI; Ref 25 abbreviated journal name expanded). Operating in Mode A, nine heuristics were applied (H8 skipped); H9 and H10 were specifically checked.

Score = 100 − (H × 12) − (E × 5) − (M × 2) [floor: 0]

For this run: 100 − (1 × 12) − (1 × 5) − (0 × 2) = 83.

v5 comparison: Under v5 formula (Score = 100 − (H×12) − (E×5) − (M×2) − (D×3)), with 1H, 0E (H10 not in v5), 0M, 29D:
100 − 12 − 0 − 0 − 87 = 1 ≈ the documented v5 score of ~5 (a different split, but the D × 3 term dominates and floors near zero).

The v6 score of 83 vs. v5's ~5 confirms the calibration intent: a largely clean article with one genuine finding now scores in the upper-middle range under v6, correctly distinguishing it from heavily-fabricated adversarial sets (which score 0). The H10 contribution (−5 for Ref 25) correctly reflects a real concern without overwhelming the score.
